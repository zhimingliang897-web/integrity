## Statistical Testing for AR(p) Models

AR(p) and MA(q) models present clear patterns in PACF and ACF, respectively. We can use them to construct our test statistics with their limiting distributions.

## Testing  $ H_{0} $ : AR(p) model

Let  $ \hat{\phi}_{kk} $  be the estimated PACF of an AR $ (p) $  series of length N. We have

 $$ \hat{\phi}_{k k}\sim\mathcal{N}\left(0,\frac{1}{N}\right)\quad\mathrm{f o r}k>p\qquad\mathrm{a s}N\rightarrow\infty. $$ 

To test  $ H_{0} $ : the process is  $ \operatorname{AR}(p) $ , i.e.,  $ \phi_{kk} = 0 $  for k > p, we use the z-statistic:

 $$ Z_{p+1}^{(A R)}=\sqrt{N}\hat{\phi}_{p+1,p+1}\qquad\mathrm{w i t h}\quad Z_{p+1}^{(A R)}\xrightarrow[H_{0}]{d}\mathcal{N}(0,1). $$ 

Reject  $ H_{0} $  if  $ |Z_{p+1}^{(AR)}| > 1.96 $  at 5% significance level.

Procedure of sequential significance tests of the PACF tail:

① Test  $ \phi_{11}=0\rightarrow $  if  $ Z_{1}^{(AR)} $  significant, not AR(2)

② Test  $ \phi_{22}=0\rightarrow $  if  $ Z_{2}^{(AR)} $  significant, not AR(2)

continue... until first insignificant  $ Z_{p+1}^{(AR)} \Rightarrow $  accept AR(p)

## Bartlett-type Tests for MA(q) Models

## Testing  $ H_{0} $ : MA(q) model

Let  $ \rho_{k} $  and  $ r_{k} $  be the ACF and sample ACF of an MA(q) series of length N. We have

 $$ r_{k}\sim\mathcal{N}\left(0,\frac{1+2\sum_{j=1}^{q}\rho_{j}}{N}\right)\quad\mathrm{f o r}k>q\qquad\mathrm{a s}N\rightarrow\infty. $$ 

To test  $ H_{0} $ : the process is MA(q), i.e.,  $ \rho_{k} = 0 $  for k > q, we use the z-statistic:

 $$ Z_{q+1}^{(MA)}=\frac{r_{q+1}}{D_{q}}\qquad with\quad D_{q}=\sqrt{\frac{1+2\sum_{j=1}^{q}r_{j}}{N}}\quad and\quad Z_{q+1}^{(MA)}\xrightarrow[H_{0}]{d}\mathcal{N}(0,1). $$ 

Reject  $ H_{0} $  if  $ |Z_{q+1}^{(MA)}| > 1.96 $  at 5% significance level.

 $$ D_{0}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{N}} $$ 

Procedure of sequential significance tests of the ACF tail:

① Test  $ \rho_{1}=0\rightarrow $  if  $ Z_{1}^{(MA)} $  significant, not MA(0)

② Test  $ \rho_{2}=0\rightarrow $  if  $ Z_{2}^{(MA)} $  significant, not MA(1)

continue... until first insignificant  $ Z_{q+1}^{(MA)} \Rightarrow $  accept MA(q)

Residual Analysis/Diagnostics

## Purpose of Residual Analysis

Consider a fitted ARMA $ (p, q) $  model

 $$ \hat{\phi}(B)(X_{t}-\hat{\mu})=\hat{\theta}(B)\hat{Z}_{t}, $$ 

 $$ \{x_{1},\cdots,x_{N}\}\downarrow based on\frac{x}{2t}=0,t\leq0 $$ 

residual analysis checks model adequacy.

 $$ \Rightarrow\{\hat{z}_{1},\hat{z}_{2},\cdots,\hat{z}_{n}\} $$ 

## Definition (Model Residuals)

The residuals  $ \{\hat{Z}_{t}\} $  are defined recursively by

 $$ \hat{Z}_{t}=X_{t}-\hat{\mu}-\sum_{i=1}^{p}\hat{\phi}_{i}(X_{t-i}-\hat{\mu})+\sum_{j=1}^{q}\hat{\theta}_{j}\hat{Z}_{t-j}, $$ 

with suitable initialization (e.g.  $ \hat{Z}_{t}=0 $  for  $ t\leq0 $ ). Residuals are estimates of the unobserved innovations.

## Working Principle

If the fitted model is appropriate, the residuals should behave like

 $$ \hat{Z}_{t}\stackrel{d}{\approx}W N(0,\sigma^{2}), $$ 

i.e., no systematic structure in residuals.

## Basic Diagnostic Checks

Residual analysis typically examines:

• Zero mean:

 $$ \frac{1}{N}\sum_{t=1}^{N}\hat{Z}_{t}\approx0. $$ 

• Constant variance:

 $$ \widehat{\mathrm{V a r}}(\hat{Z}_{t})\approx\hat{\sigma}^{2}. $$ 

• Absence of serial correlation.

## Key focus

The most important diagnostic is serial uncorrelatedness.

Heuristic Approach - Residual ACF: Let  $ \hat{r}_{k} $  denote the sample ACF of residuals.

• Plot  $ \{\hat{r}_{k}\} $  for residuals.

• Under correct specification:

 $$ \hat{r}_{k}\approx0\quad or $$ 

 $$ |\hat{r}_{k}|\leq\frac{1.96}{\sqrt{N}} $$ 

for all  $ k \geq 1 $ .

## Portmanteau Tests

Residual ACF plots are informal; formal tests are often required.

Null Hypothesis

 $$ H_{0}:\hat{Z}_{t}\sim W N(0,\sigma^{2}). $$ 

## Definition (Box–Pierce (1970) statistic, not used anymore)

For lag m,

 $$ Q_{\mathrm{B P}}(m)=N\sum_{k=1}^{m}\hat{r}_{k}^{2}. $$ 

## Definition (Ljung–Box (1978) statistic, a modified Box–Pierce statistic)

 $$ Q_{\mathrm{L B}}(m)=N(N+2)\sum_{k=1}^{m}\frac{\hat{r}_{k}^{2}}{N-k}. $$
