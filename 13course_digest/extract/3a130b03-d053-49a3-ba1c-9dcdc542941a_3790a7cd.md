## Akaike Information Criterion (AIC)

## Definition (Akaike Information Criterion (AIC))

For an ARMA $ (p, q) $  model, the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) is

 $$ \mathrm{A I C}(p,q)=-2\ell_{p,q}+\underbrace{2k}_{A I C\mathrm{~P e n a l t y}}, $$ 

where  $ k = p + q + 1 $  is the number of estimated parameters (excluding  $ \mu $  if treated separately).

## Equivalent form

Since  $ -2\ell_{p,q} \propto N \log(\hat{\sigma}_{p,q}^{2}) $ ,

 $$ \mathrm{A I C}(p,q)=N\log(\hat{\sigma}_{p,q}^{2})+2(p+q)+\mathrm{c o n s t}. $$ 

AIC favors models with strong predictive performance.

Tends to select larger models.

## Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC)

## Definition (Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC))

The Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC) is

 $$ \mathrm{BIC}(p,q)=-2\ell_{p,q}+\underbrace{k\log N}_{BIC\ Penalty}, $$ 

 $$ k=p+q+1. $$ 

## Equivalent form

 $$ \mathrm{BIC}(p,q)=N\log(\hat{\sigma}_{p,q}^{2})+(p+q+1)\log N+\mathrm{const}. $$ 

Penalty grows with sample size n.

• Strongly favors parsimonious models.

## Hannan–Quinn criterion (HQ)

## Definition (HQ criterion)

The Hannan–Quinn criterion is defined as

 $$ \mathrm{H Q}(p,q)=-2\ell_{p,q}+\underbrace{2k\log\log N}_{H Q P e n a l t y}, $$ 

 $$ k=p+q+1. $$ 

## Positioning

AIC penalty < HQ penalty < BIC penalty (for large N).

• HQ is consistent like BIC.

Penalizes complexity less aggressively than BIC.

## Consistency Properties

## Theorem (Consistency (informal))

Suppose the true data-generating process is  $  \text{ARMA}(p_0, q_0)  $ . Then, as  $ N \to \infty $ :

• AIC selects models with order  $ \geq(p_{0}, q_{0}) $  with positive probability.

BIC and HQ select the true order  $ (p_{0}, q_{0}) $  with probability  $ \rightarrow 1 $ .

## I nterpretation

• AIC prioritizes prediction.

BIC/HQ prioritize identifying the true finite-order model.

## Practical order selection procedure

• Fix a reasonable upper bound:  $ 0 \leq p \leq p_{\max}, 0 \leq q \leq q_{\max} $ 

• Fit all candidate ARMA $ (p, q) $  models.

• Compute AIC/BIC/HQ for each.

• Select the model minimizing the chosen criterion.
