## Asymptotic Distribution and Interpretation of Portmanteau Tests

## Theorem (Asymptotic distribution)

Under  $ H_{0} $  ( $ \rho_{1} = \cdots = \rho_{m} = 0 $ ) and a correctly specified ARMA(p, q) model,

 $$ Q_{L B}(m)=N(N+2)\sum_{k=1}^{m}\frac{\hat{r}_{k}^{2}}{N-k}\stackrel{d}{\rightarrow}\chi_{m-p-q}^{2},\qquad m>p+q. $$ 

• Degrees of freedom are reduced due to parameter estimation.

• Choice of m should satisfy  $ m \gg p + q $ .

## I nterpreting Portmanteau Tests

• Large  $ Q_{\mathrm{LB}}\left(Q_{\mathrm{LB}}(m) > \chi^{2}_{m-p-q,\alpha}\right) \Rightarrow \text{reject } H_{0} $ : residuals are not white noise.

• Small  $ Q_{LB} \Rightarrow $  no evidence against the model.

## Common causes of rejection

• Model order too small.

• Missing AR or MA terms.

• Inadequate differencing.

## Final Remarks: Residual Normality and Higher-Order Structure

Although Gaussianity is not required for consistency, it is often also examined by

• Histogram / Q–Q plot of  $ \hat{Z}_{t} $ 

• Normality tests (e.g. Jarque–Bera).

## I mportant Remark

Uncorrelated residuals do not imply independence. Higher-order dependence (e.g., conditional heteroskedasticity) may remain.

If residuals resemble white noise:

• Model is adequate for second-order structure.

If residuals show serial correlation:

• Revisit identification and order selection.

## Technical Recap

PACF:

 $$ \Gamma_{k}\phi_{k}=\rho_{k}\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad\phi_{k k}=\left(\Gamma_{k}^{-1}\rho_{k}\right)_{k}, $$ 

where

 $$ \Gamma_{k}=\begin{pmatrix}1&\rho_{1}&\rho_{2}&\cdots&\rho_{k-1}\\\rho_{1}&1&\rho_{1}&\cdots&\rho_{k-2}\\\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&\ddots&\vdots\\\rho_{k-1}&\rho_{k-2}&\rho_{k-3}&\cdots&1\end{pmatrix},\qquad\rho_{k}=\begin{pmatrix}\rho_{1}\\\rho_{2}\\\vdots\\\rho_{k}\end{pmatrix}. $$ 

Statistical tests for order selection:

• Reject  $ H_{0} $ : AR(p) model if  $ |Z_{p+1}^{(AR)}| > 1.96 $  at 5% significance level.

• Reject  $ H_{0} $ : MA(q) model if  $ |Z_{q+1}^{(MA)}| > 1.96 $  at 5% significance level.

 $$ Z_{p+1}^{(A R)}=\sqrt{N}\hat{\phi}_{p+1,p+1},\qquad Z_{q+1}^{(M A)}=\frac{r_{q+1}}{D_{q}}\qquad\mathrm{w i t h}\quad D_{q}=\sqrt{\frac{1+2\sum_{j=1}^{q}r_{j}}{N}} $$ 

Portmanteau (Ljung–Box Q) Tests for serial uncorrelatedness

• Reject  $ H_{0} $ :  $ \hat{Z}_{t} \sim \text{WN and ARMA}(p, q) $  model if  $ Q_{LB}(m) > \chi_{m-p-q,\alpha}^{2} $  at  $ \alpha $ -level.

 $$ Q_{\mathrm{L B}}(m)=N(N+2)\sum_{k=1}^{m}\frac{\hat{r}_{k}^{2}}{N-k}. $$
